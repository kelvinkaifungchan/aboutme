Hi there, I’m Kelvin.

I love to meet people and learn about new ways of seeing the world in the process.

I am particularly interested in process and iteration as a mechanism of resilience to respond to the contemporary complexity that surrounds us.

I grew up in Hong Kong but flew out to London to study architecture. During my time there, I collaborated with designers and engineers on various projects where I developed a specific interest in material ecology and systems thinking.

In winter of 2019, I returned to Hong Kong and joined a small architecture studio focusing on social building projects, where I learnt about pitching, designing and managing a construction project.

More recently, I have also joined a research project in Hong Kong where I am writing up the research framework and data mining for a study in the analysis of adaptability in dense urban conditions.

Currently, I am studying at the Architectural Association in London, and learning to code in Hong Kong.
